01 — Problem

Students don't lack effort. They lack the right delivery.

Validated through 51-person user research across students and parents. Distraction, overwhelm, and self-doubt — not laziness — are the real barriers to learning.

02 — Process

Full SDLC planning before writing a line of code.

Technical, financial, operational, legal, and schedule feasibility documented. Functional and non-functional requirements defined. Agile selected over Waterfall with explicit justification.

03 — Philosophy

Sensei won't let you fake it forward.

Progress is gated on genuine understanding — not right answers. Because confidence built on real knowledge is what carries people into exams, jobs, and life.

About

Builder.
Thinker.
People-first.

I'm a 2nd-year Computer Programming & Analysis student at Fanshawe College, and a Production Supervisor at Tim Hortons with 3+ years managing 8–30 person teams across a ~$3M annual revenue operation.

What drives me isn't the code. It's the gap between what technology could do for people and what it actually does. I care about the planning phase — defining requirements, understanding stakeholders, conducting feasibility analysis, and making sure what gets built actually solves the right problem.

At Tim Hortons, I run feasibility assessments on large orders daily — evaluating resource, operational, schedule, and quality constraints before committing. I didn't know that was called the SDLC. But I've been living it for years.

I'm actively targeting Associate PM, Business Analyst, and Systems Analyst internship roles for summer 2025 — ideally at companies building productivity, workflow, or learning tools where my background is a credential, not a liability.
